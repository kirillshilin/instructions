# Marketing Strategy

## When to Use

- Drafting marketing copy for web, email, social media, or advertising.
- Planning a campaign with goals, audience, channels, timeline, and KPIs.
- Defining or refining product positioning and message architecture.
- Creating a go-to-market plan for a launch or feature release.
- Building a content calendar or editorial strategy.

## Process

1. **Define the audience** — identify who the marketing is for, what they care
   about, their stage in the buyer journey, and the action you want them to take.

2. **Establish the goal** — confirm the primary objective (awareness, lead
   generation, conversion, retention) and the specific target metric.

3. **Develop positioning** — use the template:
   "For [target customer] who [need / problem], [Product] is a [category] that
   [key benefit]. Unlike [competitor / alternative], [Product] [key differentiator]."
   Build a message architecture with category claim, differentiator, value
   proposition, and proof points.

4. **Plan the campaign or content** — define:
   - Audience and segment.
   - Key message and supporting proof points.
   - Channels: primary and supporting.
   - Content types and formats.
   - Timeline with key milestones.
   - KPIs and measurement method.

5. **Draft and refine** — produce the copy, campaign brief, or content plan.
   Offer 2–3 headline or subject-line variants for testing. Explain key
   phrasing choices.

6. **Measure and iterate** — treat copy and messaging as hypotheses. Define
   what success looks like in concrete, measurable terms and plan how to
   validate it.


## Best Practices

- Lead with value to the audience, not features of the product.
- Be specific — vague claims ("best-in-class") erode trust; use evidence and
  concrete metrics.
- Match tone to channel — a LinkedIn post and a cold email require different
  registers.
- Maintain positioning consistency across all channels and materials.
- Treat copy as a hypothesis: define how you will measure whether it works.
- Every campaign should have at least one clearly defined KPI before launch.
